My mum always said, “Life should not be resigned to the status quo. It is poetic and faraway.” With this cultivation, I have grown up to be a life explorer, mentally and physically alike. In my early twenties, I have devoted great passions in travel, art, and mass communications. Of course, many other attractive things will later pop out into my life, yet my self-exploration has helped me to find out several essentials in which dance and communications are what I determine to follow. Now being a MAMC—Public Relations’ student at University of Florida , I strive to pursue a career in public relations specializing in dance and performing arts.


Art is long, but life is short. It provides me with endless inspirations and fulfillments. Ever since the primary school, I have begun to depict the world by strokes and colors. Later in the high school, I fell in love with dance and from street dance, ballroom, to ballet, I persisted this interest till now.

In my spare time, I am a constant museum-goer and a huge fan for all kinds of artisanal designs, films, and music. I also like to think and write about art issues.

My unforgettable art experiences in America include the visits of a series of art museums in New York City where I spent a whole day in the Metropolitan Museum on sketching and the enjoyment of the classic Broadway show: the Phantom of the Opera. In the summer 2013, I also volunteered as a MUSE in Samuel. P Harn Museum at University of Florida during which I developed and implemented art programming for Museum Night with students from different cultural backgrounds.

See my art reviews from Covering the Arts course in 2013


Whether in a Starbucks coffee shop, an overnight youth hostel, or a compartment of train, I like to start a conversation with the person who gives me a good first impression. This is how I explore world’s cultures and ideologies.

As I grow up, I extend this habit into more professional forms—journalism and public relations. During the undergraduate years in China, I continually reported news on campus, interned in a mainstream newspaper and a high-end cultural magazine. I was fascinated most with the stories told from different generations, ranging from freshmen to 97-year-old “living historic archive” of Chongqing City.

I also engaged actively as a strategic maker and public liaison with projects and social activities. Four years ago, I joined AIESEC, an international student-run organization for global internship exchange and potential development. Apart from marketing, business negotiations with local companies for the Incoming Exchange Function, I also launched an international project, Green Power Project in Sichuan Province, China. The latter one gained me precious experiences in management, strategic planning, coordination, promotion, and external relations.

See the outline and review of my Green Power Project, (now named ACTION!BLUE)


A famous Chinese saying goes, “Travelling thousands of miles is better than reading thousands of books.” At the age of 16, I began my first adventure in 5 European countries (Britain, France, Germany, Belgium, and Luxembourg) with the high school’s summer camp. During my undergraduate years, I went to Beijing, Chengdu, two watery towns in the south of China, and the Northwest of China and as a backpacker. So far, I have set my footprints in Japan, the south of America, and Cancun in Mexico.

Travel has indeed broadened my horizon and sharpened my wits. I became open-minded and increasingly curious about the world I haven’t touched. It makes me resolute to live in a country with diverse cultures and beliefs. This is a main reason that I came to America for my advanced education.
