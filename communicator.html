Public Relations and I


Whether in a Starbucks coffee shop, an overnight youth hostel, or a compartment of train, I like to start a conversation with the person who gives me a good first impression. This is how I explore the world.

As I grow up, this habit extends into a professional goal for public relations. In 2009, I joined AIESEC, an international student-run organization for global exchange and youth potential development. I gained precious experiences in management, promotion, and external relations from an international project, Green Power Project in which I was a regional coordinator. See the outline and review of my Green Power Project (now named ACTION!BLUE).

Apparently, I strive to make myself a public liaison in my beloved dance and performing arts industry.

My Current PR Campaign



Dance Link Click Project


Every dancer is unique, not only under the limelight, but also in the virtual world. While high technology preserves the ephemeral beauty of dance works, it also challenges dancers to establish unique dance identities outside the stage.

Also, no dancer can better thrive in a small community today. Inspirations and mutual benefits come from the world outside, especially the Internet. No matter what you are looking for—industrial information, employers’ updates, or cooperation with other dancers—an online platform with social media tools is thus necessary to maximize your artistic outcomes.

These give birth to Dance Link Click. A dance platform assists dancers to extend motion beauty to the internet and also transform online connections to offline activities and career benefits. Its ultimate goal is to enrich the dance life and develop the professional path based on each individual’s needs. See the project proposal
